FAQs

Canada has a lot of migration programs. There are a lot of ways for people to come here. If you want to study or work, the posibilities are a lot.
Here is a list by provinces:
  • Alberta
  • British Columbia
  • Manitoba
  • New Brunswick
  • Newfoundland and Labrador
  • Northwest Territories
  • Nova Scotia
  • Ontario
  • Prince Edward Island
  • Saskatchewan
And two big migration programs
  • Rural and Northern Immigration Pilot
    • North Bay, ON
    • Sudbury, ON
    • Timmins, ON
    • Sault Ste. Marie, ON
    • Thunder Bay, ON
    • Brandon, MB
    • Altona/Rhineland, MB
    • Moose Jaw, SK
    • Claresholm, AB
    • Vernon, BC
    • West Kootenay (Trail, Castlegar, Rossland, Nelson), BC
  • Atlantic Immigration Pilot

If you choose to study and then leave the country, it is not so hard to decide. You can search colleges or universities available accorging to the area of your preference.
On the other hand, if you want to come and stay in Canada after graduation, you have to take in consideration some factors, such as:
  • skills
  • education
  • language ability
  • work experience
  • other factors
Canada has a comprehensive ranking system (CRS) used to rank you in the case of asking for a permanet residency (PR).
Click here to acces the IRCC for point calculator.

You can make a list of cities you have found after narrowing the possibilities, and search it's climate, population, activities to do, work oportunities, nature... any aspect it's important for you to help decide or make easier the coice.
Once you chose one AND ONLY one city, it is time to get your papers together an apply for a visa.

All the information is on the IRCC Inmigration and citizenship page. Papers you will need to tramit the VISA an inmigration info.
